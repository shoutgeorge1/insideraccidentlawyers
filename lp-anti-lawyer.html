Most of it is marketing noise. Let's cut through it.

When You Probably Don't Need a Lawyer

Minor fender bender with no injuries? You probably don't need a lawyer. Your insurance will handle it. The other driver's insurance will cover your repairs. Move on.

Small property damage, no medical bills? Same answer. Insurance exists for this.

You're not sure if you were hurt? Wait. See a doctor. Then decide. Don't call a lawyer just because you're confused.

Lawyers cost money (even on contingency, we take a percentage). If your case is worth $2,000 and you hire a lawyer, you might end up with less after fees than if you'd just handled it yourself.

When You Absolutely Should Call a Lawyer

You were seriously injured. Broken bones, surgery, hospital stays, ongoing treatment. This isn't about a quick settlement—it's about getting what you actually need.

The insurance company is lowballing you. They offered $5,000, but your medical bills are $25,000. That's not a negotiation—that's them hoping you don't know your rights.

You're missing work and losing income. Your injuries aren't just medical—they're financial. You need someone who understands how to calculate what you've actually lost.

Someone else was clearly at fault. You were rear-ended. You were hit by a drunk driver. You slipped on an unmarked wet floor. The fault is obvious, but the compensation isn't.

In these cases, a lawyer isn't optional. It's necessary. Insurance companies have lawyers. You should too.

How This Actually Works

We work on contingency. That means:

  • You pay nothing upfront
  • We only get paid if we win your case
  • Our fee comes from the settlement or verdict
  • If we don't win, you don't pay us

This isn't charity. It's alignment. We only make money if you make money. So we have a real incentive to get you the best possible outcome.

Typical contingency fees are 33-40% of the recovery. We'll be clear about our fee structure from the start. No surprises.
