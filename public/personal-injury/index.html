FAQs

These are the questions most accident victims ask early.

How long do I have to file a personal injury claim in California?

Most California personal injury claims have a two-year deadline, but government claims and certain malpractice cases can be shorter.

What if I am partially at fault for the accident?

California uses pure comparative fault. If you are 30% at fault, your recovery is reduced by 30%, not eliminated.

Will my case go to Los Angeles Superior Court?

Most cases settle, but trial-ready preparation improves leverage and protects value if litigation is required.

How much is my personal injury case worth?

Value depends on liability strength, injury severity, treatment costs, wage loss, and available coverage.

Do I pay fees upfront?

No. We work on contingency, so there are no attorney fees unless we recover compensation. Case costs may be advanced and deducted from the recovery.
